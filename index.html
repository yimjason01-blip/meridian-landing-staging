03 — The system

A medical team large enough
for the data.

Your genome, your blood, your wearables, your history — together that's roughly 300,000 tokens of information. A physician's working memory holds about 150.

Meridian convenes four frontier AI models in a structured medical-board review of your full data picture, then hands the synthesis to a Meridian MD. The AI does what AI is good at: hold everything at once. Your physician does what physicians are good at: judgment, gut check, and a real conversation with someone who knows your name.

300,000 tokens of data in your full picture
150 tokens a physician can hold in working memory
4 models in structured debate over your interpretation
1 physician who knows your name and conducts the symphony
04 — Why this exists

I built this for myself first.

Last year I took a clinical-grade genetic test out of curiosity.

It came back with a pathogenic variant in a gene called ATM — three-fold elevated pancreatic cancer risk, plus changes to how a common statin works in my body. My doctor had never tested for it.

We ran my full data through the Meridian system. Ten clinically significant findings. My doctor would have caught three or four. The rest would have stayed invisible.

That's why this exists.
